My journey into software engineering began with a curiosity about how technology shapes the world around us. At the University of Houston, I’ve pursued a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a minor in Entrepreneurship, where I’ve explored core technical areas like operating systems, data structures, and machine learning. Outside the classroom, I’ve turned theory into practice by developing full-stack projects like CougBay, a marketplace tailored for students, where I integrated tools like Prisma, Stripe, and Resend to enhance user experience and functionality. Each line of code and architectural decision sharpened my problem-solving skills and deepened my appreciation for clean, scalable systems.

Beyond the code, I found a passion for community and collaboration. As a Front-End Developer with the Ismaili Professional Network, I built responsive web apps with AngularJS and Tailwind CSS, working closely with designers and stakeholders to bring features to life. My role as Director of Member Relations for Code[Coogs] has let me share knowledge with others—leading workshops on Bash scripting, LeetCode, and Node.js to help fellow students grow their technical toolkit. These experiences have shaped me into a well-rounded engineer who’s just as excited about solving technical problems as I am about mentoring and building with others.
